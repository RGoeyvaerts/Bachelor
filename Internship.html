Summary

My internship took place at Van Roey, a managed service provider offering IT solutions to businesses and organizations. Nearly a year into using SentinelOne, Van Roey tasked us with comparing it to Microsoft Defender to identify the best fit based on specific criteria. This project provided a deep dive into how these two Extended Detection and Response (XDR) solutions stack up against each other but also considering the size and financial situation of the customer.

Our analysis went beyond traditional Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) features, encompassing their full XDR capabilities. We began by crafting a detailed action plan. To gain a thorough understanding of Microsoft Defender and Intune (used by Van Roey for deployment simulation), we completed relevant Microsoft training modules. After getting access to both platforms we commenced our research.
We will research the following topics:

  • SKU's & Features
  • Management
  • Deployment
  • Testing
  • S1 RangerAD
  • Defender for Identity
  • Integrations
Following project completion and documentation, we presented our findings to both the Van Roey support team and the security and Microsoft teams. The final internship month involved working alongside the support team to gain firsthand experience of their daily operations and the essential role they play.
